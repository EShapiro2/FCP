Combinatorial Regulation
•This combination of multiple regulatory sites (and proteins) for a single gene allows fine-tuning of transcription and its levels
•The action of many transcription factors depends on the formation of the correct complex at the regulatory site.
•For example, some factors do not bind to DNA directly but are recruited by binding to other DNA binding proteins.
•The same protein may serve as an activator or repressor, depending on the context in which it is present
