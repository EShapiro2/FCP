Eukaryotic Transcription
•Most prokaryotic genes are controlled by only one or two gene regulatory proteins
•The regulation of higher eukaryotic genes is much more complex, where tens of transcriptional activators and repressors may bind to a large regulatory region
•For example, the control region of the eve gene of the fruit fly (Drosophila) is 20,000 nt long, and has binding sites for over 20 proteins
