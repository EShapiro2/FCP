Glycogen Branching - “Rules”
• A branch is created by
– Tranfering a 7-residue segment from the end of a chain
– To the C6-OH group of a glucose residue on the same or
another glycogen chain
– Each transferred segment must come from a chain of at
least 11 residues, and
– The new branch point must be at least 4 residues away
from any other branch points
• This branching pattern of glycogen was optimized
by evolution for efficient storage and mobilization
of glucose
